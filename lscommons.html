The Platform

Three tools. One ecosystem.

Each product is independent but deeply integrated — your notes, citations, and analytics all talk to each other.

Learn and Grow
Law School Commons
The student learning platform. AI-powered tutoring, flashcard-style topic review, professor content management, and progress tracking — all built around how law students actually study.
  • 19 core 1L topics with AI-generated quizzes
  • Multi-model AI mentor (Claude, GPT-4o, Gemini)
  • Professor document library with persona context
  • Rich-text note editor with card tagging
  • Class enrollment, study streaks, bookmarks
Legal Citation
Cite Commons
Legal citation for everyone. Paste any case, statute, or source — get a perfect Bluebook, ALWD, or APA citation in seconds. No more hunting through the Bluebook index.
  • Grounded in Indigo Book with AI training knowledge on all other citation manuals
  • Case law lookup and citation verification
  • Saved citation libraries per course
  • Auto-format entire reference lists
  • Integrated directly into Commons notes
For institutions
Admin Commons
Institutional intelligence for law schools. Track engagement, measure learning outcomes, manage faculty access, and export comprehensive analytics — all in one dashboard.
  • Student progress & engagement analytics
  • Faculty management and class enrollment
  • Citation usage and topic coverage reports
  • CSV export and custom date ranges
  • Multi-program cohort comparison
Why Commons

Built by a law school survivor
turned professor.

Every feature exists because a real 1L needed it at 11pm the night before a cold call.

AI That Knows Your Class
Your professor's documents, syllabi, and notes become the context for every AI response. Ask about a specific case, get an answer shaped by your professor's perspective.
Study When You Actually Study
Streaks, bookmarks, progress tracking, and a floating notes panel that stays out of your way. Designed for the reality of law school — not a classroom demo.
Professors Are First-Class
Professors build content, upload materials, leave feedback on student work, and see exactly who's engaging with what. Not an afterthought — a full teaching interface.
Rich Notes, Your Way
Full Quill rich-text editor for free-form notes. Card-specific notes attached to exactly what you're studying. Upload docs, link Google Drive. Your notes, in context.
One Quiz Engine, 19 Topics
AI-generated quizzes across all core 1L subjects — Constitutional Law, Contracts, Torts, Civil Procedure, Property, Criminal Law, and more. Track mastery, not just completion.
Your Institution, Informed
Admin Commons gives your law school real visibility into how students engage. Not just who logged in — who studied what, when, and for how long. Built for accreditation and beyond.
The Mission

Legal education is too important to be stuck in 2003.

Law school is one of the most demanding intellectual undertakings a person can pursue. The tools available to students haven't kept pace. LexCommons is our answer.

We believe every law student deserves a study platform that knows their professor's content, understands their subject matter, and adapts to how they actually learn — not how a product roadmap imagined they would.
